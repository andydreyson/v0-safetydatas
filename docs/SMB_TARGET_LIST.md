## üîß Vertical 1: Auto Repair Shops

### Market Size
- **US**: ~250,000 auto repair shops
- **Employees**: 1-20 per shop (target: 1-5 employees)
- **Annual Revenue**: $500K - $2M

### Regulatory Environment
- **OSHA Compliance**: Required (mechanics shops have specific standards)
- **EPA Regulations**: Hazardous waste disposal documentation
- **Inspection Frequency**: Random OSHA inspections
- **Fines**: $1,000 - $136,532 per violation

### Chemicals Used (Require SDS)
1. Motor oil and synthetic oils
2. Antifreeze/coolant
3. Brake fluid
4. Transmission fluid
5. Power steering fluid
6. Greases and lubricants
7. Parts cleaners (solvents)
8. Degreasers
9. Battery acid
10. Refrigerants (A/C work)
11. Tire sealants
12. Paints and primers
13. Welding gases
14. Fuel additives

### Pain Points

**1. Compliance Anxiety**
- Don't know what documentation is required
- Fear of OSHA inspections
- Confusion about which chemicals need SDS
- Worried about fines

**2. Disorganization**
- Paper binders that get lost/damaged
- No system for organizing 50+ SDS sheets
- Can't find sheets when needed
- Outdated information

**3. Time Constraints**
- Mechanics are busy, no time for admin
- SDS management feels like busywork
- Would rather be fixing cars

**4. Cost Sensitivity**
- Tight margins in auto repair
- Don't want expensive enterprise software
- Need something simple and affordable

**5. Technical Barriers**
- Not tech-savvy
- Complex software is intimidating
- Need something that "just works"

### Ideal Customer Profile
- **Name**: Mike's Auto Repair, Joe's Garage
- **Owner**: Hands-on mechanic, 45-60 years old
- **Employees**: 2-4 mechanics
- **Shop Size**: 2-4 bays
- **Pain Level**: High (fear of fines, disorganized)
- **Tech Comfort**: Low-Medium

### Outreach Angles
1. **Fear**: "OSHA fines for missing SDS average $3,500"
2. **Simplicity**: "As easy as uploading a photo"
3. **Cost**: "Less than one hour of labor per month"
4. **Speed**: "Set up in 10 minutes"

### Lead Sources
- Google Maps search: "auto repair [city]"
- Yelp: Automotive category
- AAA Approved Auto Repair network
- NAPA AutoCare network
- O'Reilly Auto Parts partner shops

### Estimated Conversion
- **Cold email**: 2-3% response rate
- **Trial to paid**: 15-20%
- **LTV**: $270 (18-month average retention)

## üö≤ Vertical 2: Bike Shops

### Market Size
- **US**: ~3,000 independent bike shops
- **Employees**: 2-8 per shop
- **Annual Revenue**: $300K - $1M

### Regulatory Environment
- **OSHA Compliance**: Required (if employees present)
- **Inspections**: Less frequent than auto shops
- **Fines**: Same OSHA structure

### Chemicals Used (Require SDS)
1. Chain lubricants
2. Degreasers/solvents
3. Tubeless tire sealants
4. Brake fluids (hydraulic brakes)
5. Suspension fluids
6. Greases
7. Frame cleaning solutions
8. Tire mounting lubricants
9. Threadlock compounds

### Pain Points

**1. Workshop Safety**
- Mechanics work with chemicals daily
- Small, enclosed spaces
- Ventilation concerns
- First aid protocol needs

**2. Staff Training**
- Young employees (often first job)
- Need to train on chemical safety
- High turnover = constant retraining
- SDS needed for training materials

**3. Eco-Conscious Culture**
- Bike industry is environmentally aware
- Want to handle chemicals responsibly
- Green disposal practices
- SDS helps with proper handling

**4. Community Reputation**
- Local shops rely on reputation
- Safety incidents hurt word-of-mouth
- Want to be seen as responsible employers

### Ideal Customer Profile
- **Name**: Pedal Power Bikes, The Bike Hub
- **Owner**: Enthusiast turned business owner, 35-50
- **Employees**: 2-4 mechanics, often young
- **Shop Vibe**: Community-focused, eco-conscious
- **Pain Level**: Medium (aware but not urgent)
- **Tech Comfort**: Medium-High

### Outreach Angles
1. **Workshop Safety**: "Keep your mechanics safe"
2. **Training**: "Built-in safety training for new hires"
3. **Eco-Friendly**: "Proper chemical handling for green shops"
4. **Professional**: "Show customers you take safety seriously"

### Lead Sources
- Google Maps: "bike shop [city]"
- Yelp: Bikes category
- PeopleForBikes retailer directory
- Local bike club partnerships
- Strava local segment sponsors

### Estimated Conversion
- **Cold email**: 3-5% response rate
- **Trial to paid**: 20-25%
- **LTV**: $360 (24-month average retention)

## üíÜ Vertical 3: Skin Clinics / Med Spas

### Market Size
- **US**: ~20,000 med spas and dermatology clinics
- **Employees**: 3-15 per clinic
- **Annual Revenue**: $1M - $5M

### Regulatory Environment
- **FDA Compliance**: Product documentation required
- **OSHA Compliance**: Bloodborne pathogen standards + chemicals
- **State Regulations**: Varies by state
- **Inspections**: FDA and state board inspections
- **Fines**: Can include license suspension

### Chemicals/Products Used (Require SDS)
1. Chemical peels (glycolic, salicylic, TCA)
2. Disinfectants (CaviCide, Barbicide)
3. Sterilization solutions
4. Cleaning agents
5. Numbing creams (topical anesthetics)
6. Injectable products (neurotoxins, fillers)
7. Microneedling solutions
8. Laser cooling gels
9. Chemical depilatories
10. Professional skincare products
11. Nail care chemicals (if offered)

### Pain Points

**1. Multi-Layer Compliance**
- FDA requirements for products
- OSHA for workplace safety
- State board regulations
- Insurance requirements
- Multiple inspectors to satisfy

**2. High- Stakes Environment**
- Working on people's faces
- Products applied to skin
- Allergic reactions possible
- Lawsuit exposure
- Reputation is everything

**3. Product Variety**
- Dozens of different products
- Each needs documentation
- Constantly adding new treatments
- Hard to stay organized

**4. Staff Complexity**
- Mix of medical and non-medical staff
- High turnover in support roles
- Need to train everyone on safety
- Different knowledge levels

**5. Client Questions**
- Clients ask about ingredients
- Need to provide information
- Want to appear knowledgeable
- Builds trust with clients

### Ideal Customer Profile
- **Name**: Glow Med Spa, Renew Dermatology
- **Owner**: Medical professional or entrepreneur, 35-55
- **Staff**: 1-2 providers + 2-4 support staff
- **Services**: Facials, injectables, laser treatments
- **Pain Level**: High (liability concerns)
- **Tech Comfort**: Medium

### Outreach Angles
1. **Liability**: "Protect your license and reputation"
2. **Client Trust**: "Answer any ingredient question instantly"
3. **Staff Safety**: "Keep your team safe with proper documentation"
4. **Professionalism**: "Impress inspectors and clients"

### Lead Sources
- Google Maps: "med spa [city]"
- Yelp: Medical Spas category
- RealSelf provider directory
- American Med Spa Association directory
- State medical board directories
- Local medical aesthetic groups

### Estimated Conversion
- **Cold email**: 2-3% response rate
- **Trial to paid**: 25-30% (higher value customers)
- **LTV**: $540 (36-month average retention)

## üíá Vertical 4: Hair Salons

### Market Size
- **US**: ~80,000 hair salons
- **Employees**: 2-10 per salon
- **Annual Revenue**: $200K - $800K

### Regulatory Environment
- **OSHA Compliance**: Required
- **State Board Compliance**: Cosmetology board requirements
- **Inspections**: State board + OSHA
- **Fines**: Varies by state

### Chemicals Used (Require SDS)
1. Hair color (permanent, semi-permanent)
2. Bleach/lighteners
3. Developers/peroxides
4. Hair relaxers/straighteners
5. Permanent wave solutions
6. Shampoos and conditioners (professional)
7. Disinfectants (barbicide)
8. Cleaning products
9. Nail products (if offered)
10. Keratin treatments (formaldehyde concerns)

### Pain Points

**1. Chemical Exposure Daily**
- Stylists work with dyes 8+ hours/day
- Fumes in enclosed spaces
- Skin contact with chemicals
- Long-term health concerns

**2. Client Allergies/Reactions**
- Patch tests required
- Need to know ingredients
- Allergic reactions can be severe
- Liability concerns

**3. State Board Inspections**
- Regular inspections
- Must have SDS on file
- Can result in fines or closure
- Varies by state

**4. Chaotic Environment**
- Busy, noisy salons
- No time for paperwork
- High turnover
- Stylists are artists, not admins

**5. Tight Margins**
- Competitive industry
- Booth rental model common
- Don't want extra costs
- Need clear ROI

### Ideal Customer Profile
- **Name**: Styles by Sarah, The Cutting Edge
- **Owner**: Stylist turned salon owner, 30-50
- **Stylists**: 3-8 chair rental or commission
- **Atmosphere**: Busy, social, creative
- **Pain Level**: Medium (compliance awareness varies)
- **Tech Comfort**: Medium

### Outreach Angles
1. **Health**: "Protect your stylists' health"
2. **Compliance**: "Pass state board inspections easily"
3. **Client Safety**: "Handle allergic reactions confidently"
4. **Simplicity**: "One less thing to worry about"

### Lead Sources
- Google Maps: "hair salon [city]"
- Yelp: Hair Salons category
- Salon Today directory
- Local stylist Facebook groups
- Beauty school job placement networks

### Estimated Conversion
- **Cold email**: 2-3% response rate
- **Trial to paid**: 15-20%
- **LTV**: $270 (18-month average retention)
